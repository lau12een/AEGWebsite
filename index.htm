What is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, also known as BJJ, is a form of wrestling in which the objective is to control your opponent, gain an advantageous position, and either force them to submit (typically due to a choke or joint lock) or render their offense ineffective for self-defense. Athletes compete in BJJ tournaments to test their skills against similarly-ranked, aged, and sized opponents, and scoring is based on points awarded for advancing their position. Often referred to as "human chess", BJJ demands that the practitioner learn to master employing the concepts of space, pressure, and timing against an adversary that is also attempting these same concepts.

About American Elite Grappling

American Elite Grappling (AEG) offers one of the most well rounded Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and grappling programs in the United States with over 20 gi and no gi classes offered weekly for kids and adults. Our classes focus on technique, fundamentals, and competition training. Whether you want to learn to defend yourself, compete at the highest level, improve your grappling for mixed martial arts (MMA), or simply have fun while improving your fitness, AEG will help you reach your goals. The AEG system is designed to accommodate students of all ages, experience, and fitness.



Concepts taught at American Elite Grappling:

  • Positions and awareness
  • Strength and conditioning

Ron Keslar


Ron Keslar is the head professor at AEG. Ron is a 2nd degree black belt under Leandro Vieira and started his grappling career in grade school with wrestling. Previously, Ron was a professional mixed martial arts (MMA) fighter, competing in Bellator, Strikeforce, and King of the Cage. Ron personally oversees the crafting of the curriculum at AEG including fundamentals, BJJ competition, and MMA.
